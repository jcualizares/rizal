José Rizal was a Filipino nationalist, writer, and revolutionary who is widely regarded as the national hero of the Philippines. Born on June 19, 1861, in Calamba, Laguna, Rizal was a polymath who excelled in various fields such as medicine, literature, and the arts. He was the seventh of eleven children in a well-off family and showed exceptional intellectual abilities from a young age.
José Protasio Rizal Mercado y Alonso Realonda a.k.a “José Rizal”
Philippine National Hero, Filipino political leader and author
The House of José Rizal
José Rizal's childhood home, located in Calamba, Laguna, is a reproduction of the original Spanish-colonial style house where he was born and raised. It has become a significant historical site and museum, showcasing Rizal's life and works.

The Rizal Shrine

The Rizal Shrine is the place where Rizal was born. It was typical rectangular Bahay na Bato, reminiscent of upper-class Filipino homes built during the Spanish Colonial era. The lower portion is made of adobe stone and brick, while the upper portion consists of hardwood

The shrine has a large lawn with a replica of a nipa hut Rizal used to play with his sisters in. Next to the hut is a statue of a young José Rizal, with his dog. This statue was constructed in 1996 for the centennial celebration commemorating Rizal's death.
