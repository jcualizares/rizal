Doña Teodora

Rizal's mother Dona Teodora served as his first teacher. Three years old, Rizal picked up the alphabet and prayers. After seeing her son had a gift for poetry, it was her mother who pushed him to write.

Don Francisco

Don Francisco gave Jose a huge library to expand his geographical and science knowledge. He could read the Spanish bible at five years old, and at eight he wrote a play that was successfully performed. Sa Aking mga Kabata is the title of a poem he also wrote in Tagalog.

Private Tutors

For his home education, his parents hired private tutors. Maestro Lucas Padua was the first and Maestro Celestino the second. Later on, Rizal's tutor was Leon Monroy, who also happened to be Don Francisco's old classmate. Monroy gave Rizal Latin and Spanish education.

Private School in Binan

The family chose to send Jose to a private school in Binan, Laguna where he was schooled for three years. Rizal's teacher, Maestro Justiniano Aquino Cruz, made classes for him from six to nine in the morning. His father often traveled to their house, where sometimes he gave Rizal lessons.
